What Is Keno?

Keno is a classic lottery-style casino game that has been enjoyed for centuries. Originating in China, the game spread across the world and became a staple in casinos and lottery halls. The game is simple to learn but offers plenty of excitement as you watch numbers get drawn one by one. In this free online version, you use virtual credits—no real money is involved. How to Play Keno

The Keno board displays numbers 1 through 80 in an 8×10 grid. Before each round, you select between 1 and 10 numbers by clicking on them. Once you're happy with your picks, place your bet and hit Play. The game then draws 20 numbers at random, one at a time, with a brief animation so you can follow along. Each time a drawn number matches one of your picks, it lights up. The more matches you get, the higher your payout according to the paytable.

You can adjust your bet amount before each round. Start with smaller bets while learning, then increase as you get comfortable. Use the Clear button to deselect all numbers and start fresh. The paytable shows exactly how much you win for each combination of picks and matches.

Payouts and Strategy

Payouts in Keno depend on how many numbers you picked and how many of those matched the 20 drawn numbers. Generally, hitting more matches yields bigger rewards. For example, picking 5 numbers and matching all 5 pays much more than picking 10 numbers and matching only 2. There's no strategy that guarantees wins—Keno is a game of chance. The best approach is to pick numbers you like, set a bet you're comfortable with, and enjoy the draw.

Some players prefer fewer picks for higher odds of a match; others like more picks for the chance at a big win. With 20 numbers drawn from 80, the math favors the house over time, but in a free game with virtual credits, the focus is on fun rather than profit.
