How to Play Block Puzzle

Block Puzzle is a classic falling-block puzzle game inspired by the classic falling-block puzzle genre. Colored tetrominoes drop from the top of the playfield. Your job is to rotate and move each piece as it falls so that when it lands, it completes full horizontal rows. Completed rows disappear, gems above fall down, and you earn points. The game gets faster as you level up, making it increasingly challenging to place pieces quickly and strategically.

How to Play

Use the arrow keys or WASD to move and rotate pieces. Left and right arrows move the block horizontally. Down arrow speeds up the fall. Up arrow or W rotates the piece clockwise. On mobile, use the on-screen touch buttons. A ghost piece shows exactly where your block will land, making it easier to line up perfect drops. The Hold button lets you store your current piece and swap it later — useful for saving a strong piece like the I-block for a quad clear (four lines at once).

Clear rows to score: a single row is 100 points, two rows 300, three rows 500, and a quad clear (four rows at once) is 800 points. Each 10 rows cleared advances you to the next level, and the game speed increases. The game ends when a new piece cannot spawn because the top of the board is blocked.

History & Origin

The falling-block puzzle genre originated in the 1980s and has since become one of the most beloved game concepts worldwide. The core mechanic — rotate, move, clear rows — remains timeless. Countless variants have followed, including Block Puzzle and many mobile games. The simplicity of the rules combined with the depth of strategic play has kept players engaged for decades.

Strategy & Tips

  • Keep the center flat. Avoid building a tower in the middle. Leave a flat area so you can place pieces easily.
  • Save the I-piece for quad clears. The long I-piece is the only one that clears four rows at once. Hold it until you have a full row of gaps.
  • Use the ghost piece. The semi-transparent preview shows where your block will land. Use it to align drops precisely.
  • Build a well on one side. Some players prefer a single column on the left or right edge. This lets you drop I-pieces for quad clears.
  • Don't panic at high speed. At higher levels, think one move ahead. Rotate before you move, and use soft drops (down) to save time.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a quad clear?

A quad clear is when you clear four rows at once using the I-piece (the long straight block). It's worth 800 points per level and is the most efficient way to score.

Can I hold more than one piece?

No. You can hold only one piece at a time. Once you use your held piece, you must wait for another piece to hold again.

Does the game save my progress?

Your personal best score is stored in your browser. The game itself does not save mid-game progress — each game is a single session.
