How to Play Texas Hold'em

Each player receives two private "hole" cards. Five community cards are dealt face-up on the table in three stages: the Flop (3 cards), the Turn (1 card), and the River (1 card). You make the best five-card hand from any combination of your two hole cards and the five community cards. Between each stage, there's a round of betting where you can fold, call, raise, or go all-in.

Hand Rankings

From highest to lowest: Royal Flush (A-K-Q-J-10 suited), Straight Flush, Four of a Kind, Full House, Flush, Straight, Three of a Kind, Two Pair, One Pair, High Card. When two players have the same hand type, kickers (remaining highest cards) determine the winner.

A Brief History

Texas Hold'em originated in Robstown, Texas in the early 1900s. It was introduced to Las Vegas in 1963 and became the main event format of the World Series of Poker (WSOP) in 1970. The game exploded in popularity after Chris Moneymaker — an amateur who qualified through a $39 online satellite tournament — won the 2003 WSOP Main Event for $2.5 million. The "Moneymaker Effect" sparked a global poker boom that continues today.

Strategy Tips

  • Position is everything. Acting last gives you information about what everyone else did. Play more hands from the button and cutoff, fewer from early position.
  • Don't play every hand. Beginners play too many hands. Fold weak holdings pre-flop and wait for good spots.
  • Understand pot odds. If the pot is 1,000 and it costs 200 to call, you need to win more than 1 in 6 times to be profitable. Compare this to your chance of making your hand.
  • Manage your bankroll. Don't buy in for more than 10% of your total bankroll at any single table. Variance is real — even great players lose sessions.
  • Watch your opponents. Who plays tight? Who bluffs too much? Adjust your strategy based on what you observe. Poker is a game of people, not just cards.
