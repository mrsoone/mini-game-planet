How to Play Baccarat Online

Baccarat is one of the most elegant and straightforward casino card games. The objective is simple: predict whether the Player hand, the Banker hand, or a Tie will have the highest total value. Cards are valued differently than in blackjack: Aces count as 1, numbered cards 2 through 9 keep their face value, and 10s, Jacks, Queens, and Kings all count as zero. The hand value is the sum of the cards modulo 10—so if you have a 7 and an 8, your total is 5 (15 mod 10). The hand closest to 9 wins.

Each round begins with two cards dealt to both the Player and Banker. If either hand totals 8 or 9 (a "natural"), no more cards are drawn. Otherwise, the Player draws a third card when their total is 0–5 and stands on 6 or 7. The Banker's third-card rules are more complex and depend on both the Banker's total and the Player's third card. Our game follows the standard Baccarat tableau used in casinos worldwide.

Payouts differ by bet type. Betting on the Player pays 1:1 (even money). Betting on the Banker pays 0.95:1 due to a 5% commission, which reflects the Banker's slight statistical advantage. Betting on a Tie pays 8:1, the highest payout but also the rarest outcome. Many players prefer the Banker bet for its lower house edge, while Tie bets offer excitement for those seeking bigger wins.
