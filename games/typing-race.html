How to Play Typing Race

Typing Race is a free online typing speed test that measures your words per minute (WPM) and accuracy in real time. You'll see a sentence or paragraph on screen, and your job is to type it exactly as shown. The timer starts the moment you press your first key, so there's no countdown—just focus and type.

How It Works

Each character you type is checked instantly. Correct characters turn green, mistakes turn red, and characters you haven't reached yet stay gray. A progress bar and car animation show how far you've gotten. Your WPM and accuracy update live as you type. WPM is calculated using the standard of five characters per word, so even short sentences contribute to your score.

The game includes over forty sample sentences and paragraphs of varying length, from short pangrams like "The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog" to longer tongue twisters and proverbs. Each race picks a random passage, so you'll rarely see the same text twice in a row. This variety helps you practice different letter combinations and punctuation.

Improving Your Typing Speed

Consistency matters more than raw speed at first. Aim for high accuracy before pushing for faster WPM. Many typists find that slowing down slightly actually improves their overall score because fewer errors mean less backtracking. Use proper touch typing technique: keep your fingers on the home row (ASDF and JKL;) and let each finger handle its designated keys.

Professional typists often reach 60 to 80 WPM with high accuracy. Competitive typists can exceed 120 WPM. Whatever your current level, Typing Race gives you instant feedback so you know exactly where you stand. The real-time stats let you adjust your pace mid-race if you notice your accuracy dropping.

Track Your Progress

The game saves your personal best WPM in your browser, so you can see improvement over time. Streak tracking rewards consecutive accurate completions—finish with 95% accuracy or higher to extend your streak. Session stats show how many races you've done and your best result in the current session. Zero-friction restart lets you jump into another race immediately with a single click or keystroke.

Tips for Higher Scores

  • Read ahead. Glance at the next few words while typing the current one so your brain stays ahead of your fingers.
  • Don't fix mistakes mid-race. Finish the passage first; the game tracks accuracy but continuing smoothly often yields better WPM.
  • Practice daily. Even five minutes of typing practice can improve speed and muscle memory over weeks.
  • Use the car and progress bar. They give you a quick visual sense of how much text remains, helping you pace yourself.
