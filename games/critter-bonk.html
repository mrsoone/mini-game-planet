How to Play Critter Bonk

Critter Bonk is a fast-paced reaction game where critters pop up and you bonk them before they disappear. Critters appear from holes in a 3×3 grid—your job is to click or tap them before they vanish. Each successful bonk earns you points, but watch out for bombs that will cost you! The game runs for 30 seconds, and your goal is to rack up as many points as possible while avoiding costly mistakes.

Scoring

  • 🐹 Regular critter: 1 point per hit
  • ⭐ Golden critter: 3 points per hit (3× bonus!)
  • 💣 Bomb: −5 points if you hit it—avoid these!

Golden critters are rare but rewarding. When you see that star, prioritize it—but only if you're confident you won't accidentally tap a bomb in the next hole. Bombs can quickly erase a good run, so a split-second pause to confirm what you're hitting can save your score.

Difficulty Levels

Choose Easy for a relaxed pace with fewer bombs and slower critter appearances. Normal offers balanced gameplay for most players. Hard mode increases critter speed and bomb frequency—ideal for seasoned players seeking a real challenge. As time passes in any mode, critters pop up more frequently, so the final seconds are always the most intense.

Tips & Strategy

Build combos by bonking critters in quick succession—your streak counter rewards consistency and helps you stay in the zone. The game runs for 30 seconds, so every second counts. Focus on golden critters when they appear, but never sacrifice accuracy for speed when a bomb might be lurking. Keep your cursor or finger near the center of the grid to minimize travel time. A Brief History

Pop-up bonking games have been a staple of arcades, carnivals, and amusement parks since the 1970s. The original concept featured mechanical critters that popped up from holes while players wielded mallets to bonk them before they retreated.
