How to Play Oh Hell

Oh Hell is a trick-taking card game where the challenge is bidding exactly how many tricks you'll win. The hand size changes each round — starting large, decreasing to 1, then back up — forcing players to constantly adjust their strategy. Score 10 points plus your bid if you hit it exactly; score 0 if you miss.

Rules

Deal the round's number of cards to each player. Flip the next card to set trump. Starting left of dealer, each player bids (0 to hand size). The dealer cannot bid a number that would make the total bids equal the number of tricks — someone must be wrong. Play tricks following standard rules (follow suit, trump beats off-suit). Score: hit your bid = 10 + bid points. Miss = 0.

Strategy

  • Bid low when uncertain. Winning 0 tricks on purpose is a valid and often smart strategy.
  • Count trumps to know if your high cards will actually win.
  • Watch the dealer restriction. If you're the dealer, your bid is constrained — plan for it.
