Jumping Rabbit: Endless Vertical Platformer Fun

Jumping Rabbit is a delightful vertical platformer inspired by the classic Doodle Jump formula. Take control of a bouncy rabbit and ascend through an endless sky of platforms. Your goal is simple: keep bouncing upward and never fall below the screen. The higher you go, the higher your score. It's easy to learn but challenging to master.

The game features four distinct platform types. Green platforms are your standard, reliable landing spots—safe and steady. Blue platforms move horizontally, adding a layer of timing and precision to your jumps. Brown platforms are breaking platforms; they crumble the moment you land on them, so plan your next jump quickly. Red platforms are springs that launch you extra high, giving you a powerful boost when you need to clear a gap or reach a higher platform.

Controls are designed for everyone. Use the arrow keys or A and D on your keyboard to move the rabbit left and right. The screen wraps at the edges, so you can seamlessly travel from one side to the other. On mobile devices, tap the left half of the screen to move left and the right half to move right. If your device supports it, tilt your phone or tablet to steer the rabbit using the accelerometer—a natural and intuitive way to play on the go.

Jumping Rabbit runs entirely in your browser with no downloads required. It uses a smooth canvas-based game loop for responsive gameplay. Track your personal best score and session statistics to see how you improve over time. Whether you have a minute or an hour, Jumping Rabbit offers quick, addictive rounds of platforming action.

Strategy matters as you climb. Prioritize spring platforms when you need to recover from a risky position or reach distant platforms. Avoid lingering on breaking platforms—land and jump immediately. Time your moves on blue platforms by watching their direction. The rabbit auto-bounces on every landing, so focus on steering and platform selection. Your personal best is saved locally, and session stats track how many games you've played.
