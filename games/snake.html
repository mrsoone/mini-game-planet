How to Play Snake

Snake is a timeless arcade game where you control a growing serpent across a grid. Use the arrow keys, WASD, touch swipes, or a gamepad to move your snake in one of four directions: up, down, left, or right. Your snake moves continuously in the current direction until you change it. The goal is simple: eat the food (usually shown as a colored dot or square) that appears randomly on the grid. Each time you eat food, your snake grows longer and your score increases. The game ends if you run into a wall or collide with your own body. As your snake grows, the game speeds up, making it increasingly challenging to avoid obstacles and plan your path.

History of Snake

Snake originated in the late 1970s with games like "Blockade" (1976) and "Worm" on early computer systems. It gained massive popularity in 1997 when Nokia pre-installed "Snake" on its 6110 phone, making it one of the first mobile games to reach a global audience. Millions of people played Snake on Nokia devices, and it became an icon of early mobile gaming. The game's simple mechanics—move, eat, grow, avoid—have inspired countless variants and remain beloved by players of all ages. Today, Snake continues to thrive as a web and mobile game, proving that great game design stands the test of time.

Strategy Tips

To excel at Snake, plan your route before you move. Avoid trapping yourself in corners or creating dead ends. When your snake is long, move in wide loops around the edges of the grid to give yourself room to maneuver. Try to keep the head near the center when possible, and anticipate where food might spawn. Use the "Easy" speed when learning, then switch to "Normal" or "Fast" as you improve. Near-miss moments—when you barely avoid hitting yourself—are a sign you're pushing your limits; stay calm and focus on the next safe move. Building a streak of high-scoring games takes practice, patience, and spatial awareness.

FAQ

What happens when I eat food? Your snake grows by one segment and your score increases. The game may also speed up slightly as you grow longer.

Can I use a gamepad? Yes. Snake supports standard gamepad D-pad and left stick controls for directional movement.

How is the best score saved? Your best score is stored in your browser's localStorage and persists between sessions.

What are session stats and streak? Session games count how many games you've played in the current browser session. Streak tracks consecutive games where you beat your previous score.

Why does the game feel faster as I grow? To increase challenge, the game speed scales with snake length, making late-game play more intense.
