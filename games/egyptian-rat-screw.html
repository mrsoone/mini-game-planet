How to Play Egyptian Rat Screw

Egyptian Rat Screw (ERS) is a fast-paced, reflex-driven card game where players slap the pile to win cards. Deal the full deck evenly. Players take turns flipping cards face-up onto a central pile. When certain patterns appear, the first to slap the pile wins all the cards. Collect all 52 cards to win.

Slap Rules

  • Doubles: Two cards of the same rank in a row (e.g., 7-7)
  • Sandwich: Two cards of the same rank with one card between them (e.g., 5-K-5)
  • Top-Bottom: The top card matches the bottom card of the pile

Face Card Challenge

When a face card or Ace is played, the next player must play another face card within a set number of chances: Ace = 4, King = 3, Queen = 2, Jack = 1. If they fail, the player who played the face card takes the pile. If they succeed with another face card, the challenge passes on.

Tips

  • Watch for doubles and sandwiches — train your reflexes.
  • Don't slap incorrectly — you'll give a penalty card to the pile.
  • Face card challenges create big swings. Play strategically.
