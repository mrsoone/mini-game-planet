How to Play European Roulette

Roulette is one of the most iconic casino games in the world. The spinning wheel, the bouncing ball, and the anticipation of where it will land have captivated players for centuries. European Roulette uses a single zero wheel with 37 pockets (0 and 1–36), giving it a lower house edge than American Roulette, which has both 0 and 00.

History & Origin

Roulette originated in 18th-century France. The name comes from the French word for "little wheel." Blaise Pascal is often credited with inventing an early form while experimenting with perpetual motion machines. The single-zero European wheel became the standard in Monte Carlo, while American casinos added the double zero to increase the house edge. Today, European Roulette remains the preferred version for players seeking better odds.

Strategy & Tips

  • Outside bets have lower payouts but higher win probability. Red/black and odd/even pay 1:1 and win almost half the time (excluding zero).
  • Inside bets offer bigger payouts but lower odds. A straight-up bet pays 35:1 but has only a 1-in-37 chance of winning.
  • Zero is the house advantage. When the ball lands on 0, all outside bets (red, black, odd, even, etc.) lose. Only bets directly on 0 win.
  • Manage your bankroll. Start with smaller bets and avoid chasing losses. This game uses virtual credits, so experiment freely.
