How to Play Knife Throw

Knife Throw is a tense action game where you must stick knives into a rotating wooden log. The log spins in the center of the screen, and your knives fly from the bottom toward it. Each knife that lands successfully sticks into the wood and rotates with the log. But here's the catch: if your next throw hits a knife that's already stuck, the game ends immediately. You must place every knife in an empty spot on the log's circumference.

Game Mechanics

Each level gives you a set number of knives to throw. Level 1 starts with three knives; later levels add more. Your score equals the number of levels you complete. The log rotates at a steady pace, so timing matters—throw when there's a clear gap. Stuck knives rotate with the log, shrinking the available space. Plan your throws carefully and watch the rotation to find safe angles. The difficulty ramps up as you progress: more knives per level means more precision required. Early levels teach you the basics; later levels demand split-second timing.

Boss Levels

Every five levels is a boss round. The log's rotation pattern changes: it might speed up, reverse direction, or follow a sine wave that makes timing trickier. Boss levels test your adaptability. Survive the pattern change and you'll advance with a higher score. Many players find boss levels the most exciting part of the game. The first boss at level 5 doubles the rotation speed. Level 10 reverses direction. Level 15 introduces a sine-wave pattern that speeds up and slows down. Each boss forces you to relearn the rhythm.

Tips for High Scores

Watch the log's rotation before throwing. Identify gaps and wait for the right moment. Don't rush—each knife narrows your options. On boss levels, observe the new pattern for a full rotation before throwing. Space your knives evenly when possible to leave room for later throws. Your personal best is saved, so aim to beat it each session. Some players prefer to throw when the log's rotation brings a large gap to the bottom. Others wait for specific angles. Find a strategy that works for you and stick with it.

Controls

On desktop, click anywhere or press the spacebar to throw a knife. On mobile, tap the screen. The controls are simple: one action per throw. The real skill is in reading the log's rotation and choosing the right moment to release. No complex gestures or combos—just tap when you see an opening.
