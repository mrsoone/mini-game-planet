How to Play Ludo: The Complete Guide

Ludo is one of the world's most beloved board games, tracing its origins to the ancient Indian game Pachisi. Played by millions across the globe, Ludo combines luck with strategy in a race to get all your tokens safely home. Whether you're a seasoned player or new to the game, this guide will help you master the rules and develop winning strategies.

The Basics

The Ludo board features four colored zones—Red, Blue, Green, and Yellow—each with a home area and a starting square. Each player has four tokens that begin in their home. The main track runs clockwise around the board, with a colored home stretch leading to the center for each player. The first player to get all four tokens into the center wins the game.

Starting the Game

To enter a token onto the board, you must roll a six. When you roll a six, you can either bring a new token out of your home or move an existing token. Rolling a six also earns you an extra roll. If you cannot make a legal move, your turn passes to the next player.

Safe Squares and Capturing

Certain squares on the board are safe zones—typically the starting squares and the first square of each home stretch. When your token lands on an opponent's token on a non-safe square, you capture it and send it back to their home. The captured token must roll a six again to re-enter. Landing on your own token is not allowed.

The Home Stretch

Once a token completes a full circuit of the board, it enters its colored home stretch—a path of five squares leading to the center. Only your tokens can use your home stretch. You must roll the exact number needed to reach the center; if you roll too high, your token stays put. Strategy matters: sometimes it's better to hold back a token to block opponents or to avoid being captured.

Winning the Game

The first player to get all four tokens into the center wins. In some variations, players continue until second and third place are determined. Ludo is a game of both chance and skill—while the dice determine your options, choosing which token to move and when to take risks can make all the difference.
