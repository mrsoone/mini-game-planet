How to Play 2048

2048 is an addictive sliding tile puzzle that challenges you to combine numbered tiles on a 4×4 grid. The concept is beautifully simple: slide all tiles in one direction, and when two tiles with the same number collide, they merge into one tile with double the value. Your goal is to create a tile with the value 2048 — but once you get there, the real fun is pushing past it to see how high you can score.

How to Play

Use the arrow keys (or WASD) on your keyboard to slide all tiles in that direction. On mobile, swipe in the direction you want the tiles to move. After each move, a new tile (2 or 4) appears in a random empty cell. When two tiles of the same value collide during a slide, they merge into one tile worth their combined value. The game ends when no more moves are possible — the board is full and no adjacent tiles share a value.

Gamepad controllers are also supported: use the D-pad or left stick to slide tiles. The game tracks your score (sum of all merged tile values) and your best score for the session.

History & Origin

2048 was created by Italian web developer Gabriele Cirulli in March 2014 as a weekend project. Built in JavaScript and released as open source, the game went massively viral within days, attracting millions of players worldwide. It was inspired by earlier sliding-tile games including "1024" by Veewo Studio and "Threes!" by Sirvo. Despite its simplicity, 2048 sparked an entire genre of merge-based puzzle games and remains one of the most recognized browser games ever made. Strategy & Tips

  • Pick a corner and commit. Keep your highest tile in one corner (bottom-left or bottom-right works well). Never move it away from that corner.
  • Build a snake. Arrange your tiles in a descending chain that snakes from your corner tile. This keeps high and low values organized.
  • Avoid the up key (or your anti-corner direction). If your highest tile is in the bottom-left, avoid pressing up. It pulls your big tile out of position.
  • Think two moves ahead. Before each slide, consider where the new random tile might appear and whether it could disrupt your layout.
  • Keep the bottom row full. A full row cannot be disrupted by new spawns, giving you a stable foundation to work from.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the highest possible tile in 2048?

Theoretically, the highest tile you can create on a standard 4×4 board is 131,072 (2^17). In practice, reaching even 8192 is an impressive achievement for most players.

Is 2048 a game of luck or skill?

Mostly skill. While the random placement of new tiles introduces some variance, consistent application of the corner strategy will get you to the 2048 tile the vast majority of the time.

Can you keep playing after reaching 2048?

Yes! When you create the 2048 tile, you receive a congratulations message, but the game continues. Many players aim for 4096, 8192, or even higher tiles after their first win.
